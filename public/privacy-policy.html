BrandOps Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 16, 2026

1. Who this applies to

Anyone who installs and uses BrandOps from the Chrome Web Store (or an equivalent distribution of this extension package).

2. What BrandOps stores on your device

BrandOps keeps your workspace (tasks, pipeline, content, settings, and exports you create) in Chrome extension storage on your device. That data stays on your device unless you export it or a future version syncs to a server you configure.

The main dashboard requires a connected federated sign-in (Google, GitHub, or LinkedIn).

3. Federated sign-in (Google, GitHub, LinkedIn)

BrandOps uses OAuth 2.0 with PKCE. Tokens and minimal profile data for “Signed in as …” are stored locally. The default build does not send those tokens to a BrandOps-operated backend. OAuth client IDs are supplied at build time and/or optionally in Settings; client secrets are not embedded in the extension.

Provider Use in BrandOps
Google Sign-in; name, email, avatar per granted scopes
GitHub Sign-in; name, email, avatar per granted scopes
LinkedIn Sign-in; name, email, avatar per granted scopes

4. Network requests

Besides Chrome’s own services, BrandOps may contact:

  • Google (accounts, OAuth token, OpenID userinfo) for Google sign-in.
  • GitHub (authorize, token, user and email APIs) for GitHub sign-in.
  • LinkedIn (OAuth and userinfo) for LinkedIn sign-in; a content script may run on LinkedIn pages you visit for in-product features.

We do not sell your personal information.

5. Sign out and disconnect

Dashboard Sign out clears federated sessions as implemented. Settings Disconnect removes a single provider. Removing the extension deletes locally stored data subject to browser behavior.

6. Changes and contact

We may update this policy when the product changes. The effective date above will be revised. For questions, use the support or privacy contact on the Chrome Web Store listing for BrandOps.
